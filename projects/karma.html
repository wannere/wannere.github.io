WORK ABOUT RESUME

Karma Surface Design

R.E.M. Poster
Chaparral Type Specimen

In this final project, our task was to focus on texture and explore type, shape, photos, and analogue methods. I first explored typography and photos in two separate wine redesigns, but the class unanimously agreed that I should move forward with photos. Using the texture on the back of a dumpster, I took a brand of wine called Karma and redesigned it into boxed wine with a sophisticated touch. This project also included a 3 minute pitch presentation as well as market research to influence the design.

Research

Off-premise wine purchases are increasing, while on-premise purchases are declining. Millennials are preferring to buy from liquor stores and drink before heading out to restaurants, or eating at cheaper establishments that don’t serve alcohol.

22% of wine purchases are impulse buys. The majority were based on wine that stands out on menus or store shelves.

46% of women that browse to find a purchase stated they were intrigued by labels that are “traditional, classic, or sophisticated”.

Source: Wine Spectator

Process

I started by taking photos of interesting patterns. By using different effects and adjustments in Photoshop, I found some engaging textures that I wanted to move forward with for my product.

After getting critique on my first iteration, I also explored a couple different directions based on peer feedback, like the shape on the label, the fonts I used, and the cap colors.
